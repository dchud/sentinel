Field definitions

Fields in the Canary Database (alphabetical):

Field Definition
Author(s) Identifies the author or authors of the study.
Controls According to the classification algorithm, identifies whether the study used control animals from the same population as the affected animals (Yes or No). For example, many studies compare control animals from an "uncontaminated" reference site to animals from a "contaminated" site. In this case, the control field would indicate "no", controls not drawn from the same population.
Country Identifies the country in which the study was completed.
Curator's Comments Includes any comments on the study or its methodology not addressed in other fields. There is no specific format for this field and it is not intended to include a comprehensive critique of the study.
Human studies of association Describes the strength of evidence linking the principal exposure to the relevant human health outcome, based on a Medline search. For each study, we will identify a MeSH term (National Library of Medicine's Medical Subject Headings) associated with the study citation, or a key word from the article, that represents the most specific and important environmental exposure studied. Similarly, we will select a MeSH term or key word representing the relevant human health outcome. If more than one exposure or outcome receives equal weight in the analysis, multiple terms may be used. For infectious disease studies, exposure and outcome terms may be the same. We will conduct a Medline search for the intersection of the exposure and outcome terms, limited to human studies and excluding review articles. We will classify each study entered into the database according to the number of human studies identified in this manner, using four categories: zero studies, zero to 10, 11-20, and greater than 20. This will give some idea whether the hazard is considered to pose a human health threat. However, critical evaluation of the strength of the association is beyond the scope of this effort.
Institution Identifies the institution where the study was completed.
Principal exposure The MeSH term or study key word representing the most specific and important environmental hazard studied. If more than one exposure is given equal weight in the analysis, multiple exposures may be entered.
Principal species Identifies the principal species studied. If more than one species is given equal weight in the analysis, multiple species may be entered. This field allows a search of NCBI taxonomy to identify both common and scientific species names.
PubMed Identifier Identifying number from the study's source database.
Relevant human outcome Identifies the MeSH term or study key word representing the most specific and important potential human health outcome that may occur with exposure to the agent studied. If more than one outcome is given equal weight in the analysis, multiple outcomes may be entered.
Relevant human studies Provides a link to human studies of the principal exposure and the relevant human health outcome.
Sampling According to the classification algorithm, records whether animals were sampled according to outcome (health effect) or exposure (environmental hazard). For example, in a case-control study, sampling would be by outcome, while in a cohort study, it would be by exposure. In a cross-sectional study, sampling could be by either outcome or exposure, since these are measured simultaneously.
Source The full citation for the journal or other publication in which the study appeared (e.g., title, volume, number, pages, and date for a journal citation).
Study methodology According to the classification algorithm, identifies the methodology used to study the relationship between environmental exposure and health effect. For studies with multiple methodologies, more than one may be selected. Valid entries are: Experimental, Descriptive, Ecologic, Cohort, Case-control, Cross-sectional, Review, Other.
Timing Records whether data were collected from historical records, concurrently with analysis, or repeatedly over time.
Title The full title of the study.
Unique Identifier Machine-generated identifying number internal to the sentinel database.
