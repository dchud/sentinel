Database Description

The CANARY Database facilitates the use of animal sentinel data by creating a collection of curated animal sentinel studies that will be available to human and animal health professionals via the Internet.

This relational database contains the fields that are standard for bibliographic databases such as MEDLINE. In addition, the database also includes curated information relevant to animal sentinels.

For a complete list of fields, see our Glossary.

Studies in the Database

To be included in the database, a study must meet the following criteria:

  • The study must include a "non-traditional" animal species or population, wildlife, companion and other domestic animals (e.g., livestock and poultry).
  • Either the exposure or the health effect must be judged as potentially relevant to human health.

What Curators Add

For each study, the curator has added information about the species studied, the type of hazard and outcome, and the study methodology used. For a complete list of fields, see our Glossary.

Additional Features

The database also includes links to online databases including PubMed and the NCBI taxonomy. This allows the user to search for related articles and explore possible links between animal and human health.

Suggested Uses

  • Look for associations between specific hazards and outcomes in animal populations that could be a model for cause-effect relationships in humans
  • Look for examples of animals serving as an "early warning" for particular hazards
  • Look for examples of study methodologies to investigate animal sentinel events
  • Look for linkages between animal health and human health studies.
